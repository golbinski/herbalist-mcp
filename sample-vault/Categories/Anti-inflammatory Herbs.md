# Anti-inflammatory Herbs

Inflammation is the common thread running through most chronic diseases — arthritis, cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, IBD, and neurodegenerative conditions all involve dysregulated inflammatory signalling. Herbs in this category address inflammation through multiple mechanisms, often with better tolerability than long-term NSAID use.

## Mechanisms of Herbal Anti-inflammation

Unlike single-target pharmaceutical anti-inflammatories (e.g. COX-2 inhibitors), most herbal anti-inflammatories work at multiple points in the inflammatory cascade simultaneously. This pleiotropic action may explain both their clinical effectiveness and their generally lower side-effect profile.

Key pathways targeted:

## Herbs in This Vault

### Turmeric (*Curcuma longa*)

[[Turmeric]] is the most studied herbal anti-inflammatory. Curcumin has demonstrated clinical efficacy for osteoarthritis, IBD, and metabolic syndrome. The main challenge is bioavailability — must be taken with fat and black pepper for adequate absorption.

### Ginger (*Zingiber officinale*)

[[Ginger]] complements [[Turmeric]] in anti-inflammatory formulas. While slightly less potent for joint conditions in isolation, ginger adds antiemetic and digestive benefits and has better bioavailability in food amounts. Fresh and dried forms have different emphases.

## Comparing Turmeric and Ginger

| | [[Turmeric]] | [[Ginger]] |
|--|--|--|
| Best evidence | Osteoarthritis, IBD | Nausea, dysmenorrhoea, OA |
| Key constituent | Curcumin | Gingerols / Shogaols |
| Bioavailability | Poor (needs enhancers) | Good |
| Additional actions | Liver, neuroprotection | Antiemetic, circulatory |
| Cautions | Gallstones, blood thinners | Heartburn, blood thinners |

## The Zingiberaceae Family

Both [[Turmeric]] and [[Ginger]] belong to the *Zingiberaceae* family — a family of aromatic rhizomes with a long history of use in Ayurvedic, TCM, and Middle Eastern medicine. Their co-evolution of similar phytochemical strategies (pungent resinoids, COX inhibition) makes them natural partners in anti-inflammatory formulas.

## Anti-inflammatory Herbs Beyond This Vault

- **Boswellia** (*Boswellia serrata*) — potent LOX inhibitor; particularly useful in asthma and IBD
- **Devil's Claw** (*Harpagophytum procumbens*) — strong evidence in low back pain
- **Willow Bark** (*Salix alba*) — salicin precursor; classical forerunner of aspirin
- **Cat's Claw** (*Uncaria tomentosa*) — NF-κB inhibitor; used in RA and Crohn's

## Clinical Approach

For chronic conditions (joint pain, IBD, metabolic syndrome), anti-inflammatory herbs require consistent daily use over weeks to months to show full benefit. They are not "as-needed" analgesics in the same way as NSAIDs.

Useful combinations:
- Joint pain: [[Turmeric]] + [[Ginger]] + black pepper, with meals
- IBD: [[Turmeric]] curcumin standardised extract + [[Ginger]] for motility
- Acute muscle pain: topical [[Ginger]] compress + [[Peppermint]] oil

## Preparation Principles

- **[[Teas and Infusions]]**: practical, bioavailability moderate
- **[[Tinctures]]**: convenient but curcumin is less soluble in typical tincture alcohol % than gingerols
- **Capsules**: standardised curcumin + piperine formulations give the most reliable dosing
- **[[Salves and Poultices]]**: topical applications for local joint and muscle inflammation
