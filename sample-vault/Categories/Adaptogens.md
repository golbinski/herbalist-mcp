# Adaptogens

Adaptogens are herbs (and fungi) that increase the body's non-specific resistance to stress — physical, chemical, and biological. The concept was formalised in Soviet pharmacology in the 1940s by Nikolai Lazarev and Israelis Brekhman, who required three criteria for an adaptogen: non-toxic at normal doses, broadly normalising (reducing both excess and deficiency), and non-specific in action.

## The Adaptogenic Concept

Rather than targeting a specific disease, adaptogens work through the HPA axis (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis) and SNS (sympathetic nervous system), modulating the stress response at multiple points. They tend to:

- Regulate cortisol — reducing it when chronically elevated, supporting it when depleted
- Support immune function without over-stimulating
- Improve energy and endurance under load

## Herbs in This Vault

### Immune Adaptogens

These herbs primarily strengthen and modulate immune function:

- [[Echinacea]] — immunostimulant, direct antiviral; best used in short pulses at first sign of illness
- [[Elderberry]] — antiviral via direct virion binding; also immunomodulatory; can be used preventatively in syrup form

### How They Differ

| | [[Echinacea]] | [[Elderberry]] |
|--|--|--|
| Primary action | Immune stimulant | Antiviral + immune |
| Best timing | Early acute illness | Prevention + acute |
| Key constituents | Alkamides, polysaccharides | Anthocyanins, quercetin |
| Forms available | Tincture, capsule, tea | Syrup, tincture, tea |
| Autoimmune caution | Yes | Yes (lesser) |

## Adaptogens Beyond This Vault

Many adaptogens are not covered here but are commonly used in practice:
- **Ashwagandha** (*Withania somnifera*) — HPA axis tonic, reduces cortisol, improves sleep
- **Rhodiola** (*Rhodiola rosea*) — physical and mental fatigue, altitude adaptation
- **Astragalus** (*Astragalus membranaceus*) — long-term immune tonic in TCM
- **Holy Basil / Tulsi** (*Ocimum tenuiflorum*) — stress, cognition, blood sugar

## Combining Adaptogens

[[Echinacea]] and [[Elderberry]] are an excellent pair: echinacea stimulates immune cell production while elderberry neutralises viruses directly. For preventative winter wellness, elderberry syrup daily + echinacea tincture at first symptoms is a widely used protocol.

## Preparation Principles

Immune adaptogens need adequate dosing to be effective:
- [[Tinctures]] — most reliable concentration and absorption
- [[Teas and Infusions]] — practical for elderflower; less ideal for echinacea root
- Syrups — elderberry syrup is a classic preparation uniquely suited to the berry
